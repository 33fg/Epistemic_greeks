About the Project

Mission

The Epistemic Greek Project bridges ancient Greek philosophical inquiry with contemporary epistemic theory, exploring how knowledge is constructed, validated, and understood across different domains.

Approach

We combine rigorous philosophical analysis with modern computational methods to examine epistemic structures, argumentation patterns, and knowledge representation systems.

Scope

From Socratic dialogues to modern epistemic databases, we investigate how claims, evidence, and reasoning form the foundation of knowledge systems.

Research Areas

Epistemic Objects

Foundational claims and knowledge structures that ground entities and relationships in epistemic frameworks.

Argumentative Graphs

Networks of claims, evidence, and reasoning that support or rebut knowledge claims.

Greek Philosophical Traditions

Epistemology from Plato, Aristotle, and the Stoics, and their relevance to modern knowledge systems.

Knowledge Representation

How epistemic structures can be modeled computationally while preserving philosophical rigor.
